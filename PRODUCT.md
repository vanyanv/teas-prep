# PRODUCT.md — TEAS 7 Prep

## Register

product

## Product purpose

A private, single-user study cockpit for passing the ATI TEAS 7 nursing-entrance exam. It diagnoses weak areas, builds a week-by-week plan around a target test date, and drives daily practice (topic drills, full timed mock exams, and A&P spaced-repetition flashcards), tracking readiness over time. The design serves the work: reduce friction, make the next action obvious, make progress feel earned.

## The one user

Chris — an adult returning student making a comeback into nursing. Studies in stolen pockets of time, often on a **phone**, frequently in the **evening** after a long day. Motivated but time-pressed and a little anxious about whether the effort will pay off. Not a child; not a gamified-app native. Wants to feel capable and in control, not patronized.

## Tone

Calm, encouraging, focused, adult. Plain-spoken and direct. Celebrates real progress without confetti or hype. Never childish, never clinical-cold. A steady coach, not a cheerleader and not a drill sergeant.

## Strategic principles

- **One clear next action per screen.** Chris should never wonder "what now?" The plan and dashboard always surface the single best next thing to do.
- **Mobile-first, thumb-reachable.** Primary actions sit within thumb range; quiz-taking works one-handed on a phone.
- **Progress you can feel.** Readiness vs. target and per-topic mastery are always legible at a glance; improvement is visible and motivating.
- **Honest signal.** Scores and readiness reflect reality (all-or-nothing multi-select, real timing). No vanity metrics.
- **Respect attention.** No noise, no nagging, no dark patterns. Sessions start fast and end with a clear sense of what was accomplished.

## Anti-references (what to avoid)

- Generic SaaS admin dashboards (sidebar + identical stat cards + chart).
- The hero-metric template (one giant number with a gradient accent and three supporting stats).
- Identical card grids repeated down the page.
- Childish edtech gamification (cartoon mascots, badges, confetti, streak-shaming).
- Cluttered test-prep sites buried in ads and upsells.
