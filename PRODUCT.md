product

## Product purpose

A commercial study product for passing the ATI TEAS 7 nursing-entrance exam, sold to adult learners preparing on their own. The promise is one sentence: **take one diagnostic, know exactly what to study every day.** It diagnoses weak skills, turns the results into a plan built around a target test date, and drives daily practice (guided skill lessons, topic drills, full timed mock exams, and A&P spaced-repetition flashcards), tracking readiness over time.

The competition sells a library of content and leaves the studying strategy to the learner. This sells the strategy. The design serves the work: reduce friction, make the next action obvious, make progress feel earned.

## Who it is for

Adult returning students making a move into nursing. They study in stolen pockets of time, often on a **phone**, frequently in the **evening** after a long day. Motivated but time-pressed and a little anxious about whether the effort will pay off. Not children; not gamified-app natives. They want to feel capable and in control, not patronized.

Most arrive with a test date already booked and less time than they would like. They are choosing between this and a competitor, so the product has to be legible to a stranger in about ten seconds: what it does, why it is different, what to do first.

## Business shape

Individual accounts, self-serve signup, one learner per account. Subscription is planned but not built: the profile menu carries a placeholder rather than a checkout. Nothing in the product should promise a paid tier that does not exist yet.

## Tone

Calm, encouraging, focused, adult. Plain-spoken and direct. Celebrates real progress without confetti or hype. Never childish, never clinical-cold. A steady coach, not a cheerleader and not a drill sergeant. Marketing copy holds the same voice as the app: no growth-hack urgency, no exclamation points, no claims the product cannot keep.

## Strategic principles

- **One clear next action per screen.** A learner should never wonder "what now?" Today always surfaces the single best next thing to do.
- **Mobile-first, thumb-reachable.** Primary actions sit within thumb range; quiz-taking works one-handed on a phone.
- **Progress you can feel.** Readiness vs. target and per-skill mastery are legible at a glance; improvement is visible and motivating.
- **Honest signal.** Scores and readiness reflect reality (all-or-nothing multi-select, real timing, confidence-weighted mastery). A lucky guess is a gap, not a strength. No vanity metrics.
- **Show the product, not a pitch.** Marketing surfaces render the real interface with fixture data rather than stock photography or feature cards.
- **Respect attention.** No noise, no nagging, no dark patterns. Sessions start fast and end with a clear sense of what was accomplished.

## Anti-references (what to avoid)

- Generic SaaS admin dashboards (sidebar + identical stat cards + chart).
- The hero-metric template (one giant number with a gradient accent and three supporting stats).
- Identical card grids repeated down the page.
- Childish edtech gamification (cartoon mascots, badges, confetti, streak-shaming).
- Cluttered test-prep sites buried in ads and upsells.
- Course-library marketing: stock nursing photography, instructor headshots, "50+ hours of video" as the pitch.
